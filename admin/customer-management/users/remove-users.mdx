Learn how to remove members from the community, delete nonmembers, and reinvite past users back as members.

Administrators can remove any user from their view. Depending on the user's [role](/admin/administration/administrators/permissions), the action takes a different path:

- Removing a **member** moves them to **Nonmembers** — their profile and history are preserved at the location.
- Deleting a **nonmember** removes their profile from the location entirely.

## Remove a member

If a user is no longer your [member](/admin/customer-management/users/members) and you need to remove them from the **Customers** page, follow these steps:

1. Go to the **Customers** page in the necessary location and switch the filter to **Members**.
2. Open the user's profile, or use the row's `…` menu directly from the list.
3. Press the `…` menu in the top right of the profile (or in the list row) and select `Remove`.
4. Confirm your choice.

### What happens when you remove a user from the Customers page

- The user becomes a [nonmember](/admin/customer-management/users/nonmembers) and appears under **Customers → Nonmembers**.
- The user's **active personal subscription** at this location is cancelled immediately, with today's date set as the end date. See [Cancel a subscription](/admin/billing/subscriptions/cancel-a-subscription).
- The user's **payment methods** remain attached to the profile for any further charges that may be needed.
- The user's **billing details** remain on the profile.
- The full history of the user's **credits, day passes, payments, invoices, bookings, and activities** is preserved on the profile and continues to be visible to administrators.
- The user keeps access to any [companies](/admin/customer-management/companies/set-up-companies) they belong to — their company membership and any active company subscription are not affected.
- Administrators can [restore the user as a member](#reinvite-a-user) at any time without re-entering their information.

### Admin rights when a member is removed

When a user is moved to **Nonmembers** at a location, RNTOR also revokes their administrator/staff access at the same merchant, unless they are the **Merchant Owner / Super Admin**.

- Non-owner staff access is **deactivated automatically** for the user across the merchant. They lose the ability to sign into the dashboard for that merchant until access is re-granted.
- **Owners and Super Admins are never affected** by this automation, regardless of their member status.
- The change is recorded on the user's activity timeline as **Staff access revoked on remove**, so administrators can audit who lost access and why.
- To restore access, [reinvite the user as a member](#reinvite-a-user) and re-grant admin access from [Manage administrators](/admin/administration/administrators/administrators).

<Info>
  RNTOR currently has a single merchant-wide staff role. Revoking access here applies to the whole merchant — not just the location where the user was removed. If the user needs to keep dashboard access at a different location of the same merchant, do not remove them as a member there.
</Info>

## Auto-remove members

Auto-remove members is an optional behavior that keeps your **Members** list aligned with active subscriptions: when a member's subscription ends or expires, RNTOR moves them to **Nonmembers** automatically — no manual cleanup required.

### Configure Auto-remove members

1. Open the location and go to **Settings → Billing → Subscriptions**.
2. Toggle **Auto-remove members** on.
3. The change is saved instantly and applies to future expiries.

### How members are removed

When the toggle is **on**, RNTOR runs a daily background job that checks every member at the location and demotes them to **Nonmembers** if their subscription has ended or expired.

- A member is moved to **Nonmembers** only when they no longer have any active subscription that keeps them a member at this location (see [Personal vs company subscriptions](#personal-vs-company-subscriptions)).
- Demotion uses the same effect as a manual remove: the profile, history, payment methods, and billing details are preserved, and the user can be [reinvited](#reinvite-a-user) at any time.
- Each automated removal is logged on the user's activity timeline as **Auto-removed member**, with the reason set to `subscription_expired`.
- The job is **idempotent** — running it multiple times yields the same result.
- **Merchant Owners and Super Admins are never auto-removed**, even if they no longer hold an active subscription.

When the toggle is **off**, members keep their member status after their subscription ends. Administrators can still remove them manually from the **Customers** page.

### Personal vs company subscriptions

Auto-remove evaluates personal and company subscriptions together to avoid demoting users who are still entitled to be members through their company.

- **Personal subscription expires, no company:** the user is moved to **Nonmembers**.
- **Personal subscription expires, but the user belongs to a company with an active company subscription at the location:** the user **remains a member**.
- **Company subscription expires:** the company is demoted to **Nonmembers** at the location, and each company member is then re-evaluated. Members who have no other active subscription at the location are moved to **Nonmembers**; members who still hold an active personal subscription remain members.

Staff access revocation described in [Admin rights when a member is removed](#admin-rights-when-a-member-is-removed) also applies to auto-removals.

## Delete a nonmember

Administrators can delete nonmembers from a location when their profile is no longer needed.

1. Go to the **Customers** page in the necessary location.
2. Switch the filter at the top to **Nonmembers**.
3. Open the nonmember's profile.
4. Press the `…` menu in the top right and select `Delete`.
5. Confirm your choice.

The user can still appear again if they perform any action at this location — such as making a booking, payment, or order — at which point a fresh nonmember profile is created.
</Info>

## Reinvite a user

RNTOR keeps every nonmember who has ever interacted with your location available on the **Customers** page. Administrators can promote a nonmember back to **Members** without retyping their information.

<Info>
  You can reinvite only users who appear on the **Customers → Nonmembers** list. Users whose nonmember profile has been [deleted](#delete-a-nonmember) need to be added again from scratch.
</Info>

To make a user who appears among nonmembers your member immediately:

1. Go to the **Customers** page and switch the filter at the top to **Nonmembers**.
2. Open the profile of the user you want to restore.
3. In the **"This user is a nonmember"** banner at the top of the profile, press `Invite as member`.

  ![Screenshot Placeholder Invite As Member](/images/placeholder-invite-as-member.png)

Once invited:

- The user immediately appears on **Customers → Members** at this location.
- The user receives an email invitation to access the member web portal or mobile app.
- All their previous history — invoices, payments, credits, day passes, bookings, billing details, payment methods, internal notes, and attachments — remains intact.
- Administrators can now [create or edit a subscription](/admin/billing/subscriptions/edit-a-subscription) and manage other member properties on the profile.
