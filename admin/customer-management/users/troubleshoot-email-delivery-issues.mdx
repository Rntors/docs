title: "Troubleshoot email delivery issues    "
---

Sometimes your members may not receive emails from the member web portal or mobile app. This guide explains how to troubleshoot delivery issues with invitations, invoices, and login codes.

## Invitations to join

If a member cannot find their invitation, open the **Customers** page, select the user, and inspect the **Invitation** section.

If the email wasn't delivered, you will see a red error message:

> The invitation email to [_user@example.com_](mailto:user@example.com) couldn't be delivered. Please verify the address and suggest the user check spam or contact their email provider.

If you do not see an error message, the email was accepted by the provider and the issue is on the recipient's side.

### If you see an error, follow these steps

1. Make sure the invitee's email address is correct. If you made a typo, [remove the user](/admin/customer-management/users/remove-users) and reinvite with the correct email.
2. If the address is correct, the recipient's mail provider rejected the email. Ask the member to contact their IT team or mail provider and whitelist emails from `noreply@rntor.com`.
3. For ongoing deliverability problems, ask RNTOR support to configure a branded sender for your workspace (see [Custom branded sender](#3-custom-branded-sender)).

Use the **Resend invite** button to send the invitation again after the issue is resolved.

### If you do not see an error, try these tips

- Ask the user to check their spam, promotions, and other folders.
- Ask them to whitelist `noreply@rntor.com`.
- Reinvite them using an alternative address (e.g. `@gmail.com`, `@outlook.com`).

## Invoices

If a member cannot find an invoice in their inbox:

- Ask the user to check the inbox tied to their RNTOR account (visible on their **Account** page).
- Confirm the invoice was sent from **Billing → Invoices → Send an invoice**.
- For company invoices, confirm a [company manager](/admin/customer-management/companies/set-up-companies#add-company-members-and-managers) is assigned and has a valid email.

## Login codes

If a user is not receiving the one-time login code:

1. Ask them to check the **Promotions** or **Spam** folders.
2. Ask them to whitelist `noreply@rntor.com` with their mail provider.
3. If they are already logged in on another device, ask them to check the push notification on that device.

## Improve email deliverability

Most delivery problems are caused by recipient mail filters. The following improve inbox placement.

### 1. Whitelist the email address

If the customer receives emails in the **Spam** folder, ask them to whitelist `noreply@rntor.com` in their provider:

- [Gmail instructions](https://support.google.com/mail/answer/1366858)
- [Outlook instructions](https://support.microsoft.com/office/safe-senders-470d4ee6-e3b6-402b-8cd9-a6f00eda7339)
- [Yahoo instructions](https://help.yahoo.com/kb/SLN28140.html)

### 2. Customize the email footer

Personalize the footer of transactional emails with your business name, address, and legal information. This improves trust and reduces the chance of being flagged as spam.

### 3. Custom branded sender

RNTOR can send your transactional emails from a branded address such as `hello@yourdomain.com` instead of the default `noreply@rntor.com`.

This is currently **set up by RNTOR support** — it is not self-serve.

How it works:

1. [Contact our support team](mailto:support@rntor.com) and request a branded sender. Provide the email address you would like to use.
2. Support adds the domain to our email provider and returns a set of DNS records (typically SPF, DKIM, and MX entries).
3. Add the DNS records at your domain provider (Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap, etc.).
4. Notify support once the records are added. We verify the domain and switch your workspace to the branded sender.
5. From that point onward, invitations, invoices, receipts, and other transactional emails are sent from your branded address.

If the domain is not yet verified, emails continue to be sent from the default `noreply@rntor.com` address — no email is ever sent from an unverified domain.
